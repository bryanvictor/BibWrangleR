Travelling Culture: The Role of Migration in Imaginative Construction of Culture

Author: Dwarak, Irina1; Leelaram, Sunanda1 Vedanta Cultural Foundation, Mumbai

http://proxy.lib.umich.edu/login?url=http://search.proquest.com/docview/1438557293?accountid=14667

Abstract: The paper looks at the construction of culture in relation to the social phenomenon of migration. It focuses on the phenomenon of forced mass migration of Jewish students from the Russian Empire to Western Europe in the beginning of the 20th century. The paper views migration as the trigger for cultural, political and socioeconomic transformations among migrants. These transformations were brought about by a radical rethinking of homeland, nation, culture and identity and, as a result of which, a new powerful culture emerged. The paper describes culture as an imaginative construction. Culture is thus not a static and fixed entity contained within strictly defined boundaries of state, nation, religion, traditions and origin, but is subject to new definitions in changing socio-political contexts. Author: Raghavan, Vijay11 Centre for Criminology and Justice, School of Social Work, Tata Institute of Social Sciences, Mumbai

http://proxy.lib.umich.edu/login?url=http://search.proquest.com/docview/1438554185?accountid=14667

Abstract: This article is based on a study of male youth arrested for extortion and other gang related crimes in Mumbai. The study explores the factors and processes, which influence the entry of these youth into crime, and the attempts made by them to get out of the criminal nexus. The article delineates factors such as the area of residence, the work place, and the prison as 'criminalising sites'; and the presence of 'manifest factors' such as conflict within the family, dropping out of school, peer influence, lifestyle problems, addictions and personality factors which initiate youth into a life of crime. The study also highlights the role that the manifest factors play in the 'getting out' process, and the role of the family, legal income, and agency, in shaping the futures of the youth. Abstract: The paper explores the impact of HIV/AIDS on the food consumption pattern of households through empirical research and comparative analysis. Violence as a Consequence of Being HIV Positive: The Experiences of Women Infected with HIV and the Need for Interlocutor Policy

Author: Kagal, Neha

http://proxy.lib.umich.edu/login?url=http://search.proquest.com/docview/1622291017?accountid=14667

Abstract: This paper examines the intersections of HIV and intimate partner violence post disclosure of HIV positive status among women in Mumbai, India. It is based on the researcher's experience of working with and consequently interviewing HIV positive women who were accessing either anti retroviral (ARV) treatment in government hospitals or accessing counselling for domestic violence. During the interviewing process the researcher used a semi structured questionnaire and the life history method for data collection. Women reported various forms of violence both pre disclosure as well as post disclosure; however, the nature of violence, its intensity as well as triggering factors changed post sero-status disclosure. There were many points of interconnection between the lives of women battling both domestic violence, as well as HIV. However, neither the Prevention of Domestic Violence Act, nor the National AIDS Control Programme III addresses these points of intersectionality. It is imperative that existing services for HIV take up issues of domestic violence and vice versa, thus developing what the researcher refers to as 'interlocutor' policy. Abstract: This study examines the relationship between socio-demographic variables and perceived social support of 1,166 adolescents in Turkey. The Provision of Social Relations Scale (PSR) was used as a data collecting instrument and translated into Turkish. Abstract: The purpose of this study was to determine the effects of bureaucratisation on the teacher morale of elementary schools in central Denizli, Turkey. The sample comprised 436 randomly selected primary school teachers. The data was collected by administering the Elementary Schools Bureaucracy Level Scale developed by E. Gaye Ermec and the Elementary School Teachers Morale Survey. Regression Analysis was used to determine the impact of bureaucracy on teachers' morale. The results showed that bureaucracy has a significant impact on teacher morale. Understanding Attitudes of HIV/AIDS Counselling Trainers

Author: Sharma, Poonam11 Research and Documentation Unit, Saksham, GFTAM Round 7, HIV/AIDS Counselling Programme, Tata Institute of Social Sciences, Mumbai

http://proxy.lib.umich.edu/login?url=http://search.proquest.com/docview/1622290964?accountid=14667

Abstract: The article aims to understand trainers' attitudes on gender, sexuality, marginalisation, vulnerability and risk, and shift in trainers' attitudes as a result of a comprehensive programme intervention. The article is based on the qualitative quasi experimental study of 705 professionals associated with Saksham's HIV/AIDS counselling programme. These professionals were assessed on the global assessment of master trainers (GMaT), a tool developed by Saksham. Out of these trained 705 trainers, a sample of 224 was taken and shift in attitudes post intervention was assessed. Those trainers who attributed vulnerability and risk to individual factors were more blame focused in their responses whereas those who attributed it to structural/environmental factors were more task oriented in their responses. A shift in attitudes from pre-occupation with morality and judgment to task-oriented responses was seen. The attitudes relating to gender, sexuality and marginalisation are deep rooted and fundamentally linked to trainers' value systems, understanding of human behaviour and class. For that reason, continuous engagement and capacity building is essential to bring about critical change. Training curriculum should be structured in a manner that allows for critical reflection and engagement in very personal ways. Adapted from the source document.
